# Product Vision — Bhutan Luxe

**Status:** Pre-build. The repo contains a brand style guide (April 2026) and empty HTML scaffolds. No live site yet.

---

## What it is

A lead-capture marketing site for **bespoke luxury group journeys to Bhutan**, sold to high-net-worth travellers (initial focus: Texas). The site's job is to (1) create desire through brand-led storytelling and immersive photography, (2) present three pricing tiers, and (3) capture qualified inquiries.

Fulfilment — itinerary design, on-the-ground logistics, guides — is handled by an affiliate partner inside Bhutan. This site is the demand surface, not the operations system.

---

## Audiences

### 1. The HNW couple, 50–70

The primary buyer. Already takes 2–4 international trips a year. Has been to Europe, Africa, maybe South America. Wants what the next group of friends hasn't done yet. Bhutan reads as rare, intentional, and meaningful — not adventure-tourism. Often referred by a friend or sees the brand at a private event.

### 2. The travel-curious, 35–55

Higher disposable income, smaller party (couple or solo). Drawn to wellness, cultural depth, and slow travel. May enter at the entry tier (Boutique-Luxe) before upgrading.

### 3. Affiliates and partners

Concierge services, private banks, lifestyle advisors who refer their clients. The brand itself has to feel premium enough that they're proud to refer.

---

## Values, ranked

1. **Rarity and access.** The product is a small-group journey to a country most travellers will never visit. Rarity is the offer.
2. **Brand fidelity.** Every surface — typography, photography, prose — must read luxe. A stock-photo splash kills the offer.
3. **Frictionless inquiry.** A buyer ready to ask should be one click and one form from doing it.
4. **Affiliate trust.** The site must convey sufficient legitimacy that a wealth manager or concierge service feels safe referring a client.

---

## Moat

- **Affiliate partner inside Bhutan.** Operations are not commodity; the on-the-ground partner is the actual product.
- **Brand intentionality.** The brand style guide is mature; most luxury travel sites are not. Visual discipline is defensible.
- **Curated cohort.** Group size is intentionally small, which the buyer experiences as exclusivity and the operator experiences as quality control.

---

## Business model

| Tier | Price band | Volume target | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boutique-Luxe | $8K–$12K / person | Highest volume | Lower margin |
| Luxe | $15K–$22K / person | Mid volume | Strong margin |
| Ultra-Luxe | $30K–$35K+ / person | Lowest volume | Highest margin |

Revenue is concentrated in a few high-margin trips per year; the site does not need to be a high-volume conversion machine. It needs to convert the right person at the right tier.
